## Posting Strategy

### Frequency
- **Ideal**: 3-5 posts per week
- **Minimum**: 2 posts per week (Silver tier requirement)
- **Maximum**: 1 post per day (avoid over-posting)

### Best Times to Post (B2B)
- Tuesday-Thursday: 9 AM - 11 AM (timezone aware)
- Tuesday-Thursday: 12 PM - 2 PM
- Avoid: Weekends, late evenings, Mondays before 10 AM

### Content Mix (80/20 Rule)
- **80% Value**: Education, insights, helpful content
- **20% Promotion**: Services, achievements, offers

---

## HITL Approval Thresholds

### Auto-Approve (Low Risk)
- Generic business tips
- Industry news sharing
- Motivational content
- No specific client mentions
- No pricing or offers

### Requires Approval (Medium Risk)
- Posts mentioning clients (even if anonymized)
- Posts with metrics/revenue numbers
- Posts announcing new services
- Posts with external links
- Posts with images/media

### Requires CFO/CEO Approval (High Risk)
- Posts mentioning specific revenue figures
- Posts announcing partnerships
- Posts with legal/compliance implications
- Crisis communication
- Competitive positioning statements

## Content Guidelines

### DO ‚úÖ
- Lead with value for the reader
- Use data and specific examples
- Include engaging hook in first line
- Add 3-5 relevant hashtags (at end)
- Ask questions to drive engagement
- Tag relevant people/companies (with permission)
- Use line breaks for readability
- Include call-to-action
- Proofread for typos/errors

### DON'T ‚ùå
- Brag without backing it up
- Use clickbait or misleading hooks
- Post during late night/off hours
- Use more than 8 hashtags
- Write walls of text (use bullets/spacing)
- Post identical content repeatedly
- Copy competitors' posts
- Overpromise results
- Use jargon without explanation
- Post angry/negative content

## Crisis Management

### If Post Gets Negative Attention
1. **Don't delete** (looks guilty) - unless factually wrong
2. **Don't argue** in comments
3. **Acknowledge** valid criticism professionally
4. **Take offline** if escalating ("Let's discuss via DM")
5. **Flag for CEO** if brand risk

### When to Delete a Post
- Factual errors that mislead
- Accidentally confidential information
- Offensive content posted in error
- Client requests takedown (contract issue)

- Posts with controversial opinions
- Posts claiming specific ROI/results
- Posts mentioning regulations/compliance
- Posts about employee matters
